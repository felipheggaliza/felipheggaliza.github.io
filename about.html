About me

Hey, thanks for checking out my page. I'm Carlos Fenollosa, a Computer Engineer, and I design and develop software engineering projects

I worked for 8 years analyzing bioinformatics data and writing tools and algorithms. I then took some sabbatical time, during which I started some projects, learned UI development, electronics and telecommunications, studied a second foreign language (Chinese) and founded a startup, Paradoxa.

I am currently taking the CEO role which means that, as an engineer, I've had to learn many new skills: business development, sales, management, administration and finances. It's a whole new world, and a very interesting one; businesses are a big pillar of today's world, and I believe that a deep understanding of how they work complements well the technical world and lets you grow as a person.

Paradoxa aims to facilitate communications by using AI bots and alternative technologies to the Internet. Our first product is is Puput, a service to access email without the internet, via missed calls. I'm really proud of it, and honestly think it has a lot of potential, both for consumers and businesses. It's free, so go ahead and try it!

Personal projects

As personal projects, I write on my blog (the blog software was also written by me as a fun project). If you like reading about the linux terminal, scripting and automation, keyboard layouts and technology hacks, you should subscribe to it.

I also develop my own operating system and I'm writing a tutorial along the way.

When I was a kid, I learned to program by reading books and magazines, but the future of learning is video. I've always very interested in education, and programming will probably be the most requested skill for the next 50 years. That's why I've created a series of videos, aimed at kids and adults who wish to program but don't want to deal with all the computing complexity: Aprende a programar (in Spanish)

I also collect funny papers and run some podcasts, in Spanish: Dame la voz (political debate), 5 minutos con Carlos Fenollosa (tech), and El Amuleto de Yendor (tech, now finished).
